What is the FSCA?

The Financial Sector Conduct Authority (FSCA) is South Africa's market conduct regulator of financial institutions. It was established by the Financial Sector Regulation Act (FSR Act) No. 9 of 2017 and replaced the Financial Services Board (FSB) on 1 April 2018.

The FSCA's mandate is to enhance and support the efficiency and integrity of financial markets, protect financial customers by promoting fair treatment by financial institutions, and promote financial education and literacy.

eStokvel & the FSCA

Stokvel schemes in South Africa operate under the Banks Act Exemption Notice of 1990, which recognises informal savings clubs as distinct from regulated banking activities. As such, eStokvel — as a platform facilitating stokvel operations — is not required to hold a Financial Services Provider (FSP) licence for the facilitation of stokvel contributions and payouts.

However, eStokvel voluntarily aligns its practices with FSCA conduct standards to ensure:

  • check_circleFair treatment of all platform users
  • check_circleTransparent disclosure of fees and terms
  • check_circleAdequate complaint resolution mechanisms
  • check_circleProtection of client funds and data
  • check_circleCompliance with all applicable financial legislation

NASASA Membership

eStokvel is a proud member of the National Stokvel Association of South Africa (NASASA) — the recognised industry body for stokvel organisations in South Africa. NASASA membership confirms that eStokvel meets the standards for responsible stokvel facilitation and is committed to the growth and protection of the stokvel industry.
