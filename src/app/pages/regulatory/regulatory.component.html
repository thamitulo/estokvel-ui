Regulatory Framework

Understanding the legal and regulatory environment that governs stokvels and eStokvel in South Africa.

Regulatory Overview

Stokvels in South Africa are recognised and governed under several pieces of legislation. eStokvel operates within this framework and is committed to full regulatory compliance to protect its members.

info

eStokvel is not a registered financial services provider. Stokvel groups are informal savings structures and do not fall under the National Credit Act (NCA) or the Financial Advisory and Intermediary Services (FAIS) Act.

Key Legislation

gavel

Exemption Notice (1990)

The Banks Act Exemption Notice of 1990 explicitly exempts stokvel schemes from the definition of "the business of a bank", allowing informal savings clubs to operate legally without a banking licence.

shield

POPIA (2013)

The Protection of Personal Information Act governs how eStokvel collects, stores and processes member data. We are fully POPIA-compliant. Learn more →

policy

Consumer Protection Act

The Consumer Protection Act (No. 68 of 2008) protects consumers in transactions and ensures fair, transparent business practices. eStokvel's terms are designed to comply fully.

account_balance_wallet

Financial Intelligence Centre Act (FICA)

FICA requires us to verify member identities (Know Your Customer — KYC) to prevent money laundering and financial crime. All members are required to complete identity verification.
