Stokvel Basics

Everything you need to know about stokvels — South Africa's most beloved savings tradition, now digitised.

What is a Stokvel?

A stokvel is a type of informal savings club or credit association where a group of people agree to contribute fixed amounts at regular intervals. The pooled funds are then given to one member at a time in rotation.

Stokvels have been part of South African culture for generations, rooted in the concept of ubuntu. eStokvel brings this powerful tradition into the digital age with transparency, security and convenience.

Types of Stokvels

sync_alt

Rotating Stokvel

Each member receives the full pot in rotation. Simple, fair and time-tested.

shopping_cart

Grocery Stokvel

Members save throughout the year and make a big grocery purchase together, usually around December.

trending_up

Investment Club

Members pool funds and invest together in shares, property or other assets for long-term growth.

favorite

Burial Society

Members contribute monthly to a fund that covers funeral expenses for members and their families.

celebration

Social Stokvel

Combines savings with social events — members take turns hosting gatherings funded by the pot.

savings

General Savings

A flexible structure where everyone saves towards a shared goal, paid out at cycle end.
